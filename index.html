Agency Funding Program

Georgetown awards discretionary grants to outside non-profit and community organizations each fiscal year. Any recommendation by the Mayor is subject to availability of funds and requires City Council approval. This is separate from federal/state grants the city applies for directly.

1
Application Opens
City publishes Agency Funding Application, Budget Request Form, and Conflict of Interest Certification. Applications open annually — FY26 deadline was April 25, 2025 at 4:00pm.
2
Mayor Reviews
Mayor Jenkins reviews all applications. No funding is guaranteed. Recommendations are made based on availability of funds and alignment with city priorities.
3
Council Approves
Any agency funding recommended by the Mayor must be approved by City Council. Approved agencies execute a written agreement with the City before receiving funds.
4
Disbursement
Funds are disbursed under the written agreement for the fiscal year (July 1 – June 30). Agencies must report on use of funds per the agreement terms.

Toyota — $204.4M Hybrid Expansion · 82 new jobs
New machining line for 4-cylinder hybrid engines · Production 2027 · Part of $912M multi-plant initiative
Toyota 82 Jobs
Toyota Motor Manufacturing Kentucky (TMMK) is installing a new machining line for 4-cylinder hybrid-compatible engines at their Georgetown powertrain facility. The investment is part of a larger $912M multi-plant initiative across five states (KY, WV, MS, MO, TN) creating 252 total jobs — Georgetown's share is 82. Governor Beshear announced the investment November 20, 2025. Production on the new line begins 2027.

Toyota Infrastructure

Georgetown Commons — Mixed-Use Development
Bringardner farm · 56,585 sq. ft. grocery + retail · Frankfort Pike & McClelland Circle
Preliminary Approved
The Georgetown-Scott County Planning Commission approved the preliminary subdivision plan for Georgetown Commons, a mixed-use development on the Bringardner farm at Frankfort Pike and McClelland Circle. The plan includes a 56,585 sq. ft. grocery store with an attached liquor store, plus a 7,000 sq. ft. retail tenant building on 12.1 acres. Four new access points are planned: two to Frankfort Pike, one from McClelland Circle, and one to Fincastle Drive. A traffic light would likely be required at the Elkhorn Crossing School intersection — final determination from the Kentucky Transportation Cabinet.

Triple Crown Regional Business Park
Multi-county park · Scott + Fayette counties voted yes · Richmond opted out
Regional Jobs TBD
The Triple Crown Regional Business Park is a multi-jurisdictional economic development project spanning Scott, Fayette, and Madison counties. Scott County and Fayette County voted to participate. Richmond (Madison County) voted no, but the remaining partners agreed to absorb the $500,000 gap Richmond's withdrawal created. The park is intended to attract large-scale industrial and logistics employers to Central Kentucky. Details on total acreage, targeted industries, and infrastructure investment are being finalized.

ℹ️About TMMK

Toyota Motor Manufacturing Kentucky (TMMK) in Georgetown is Toyota's largest production facility globally, employing approximately 10,000 team members. Since opening in 1988, over 15 million vehicles have rolled off its assembly lines — including the Camry (America's best-selling sedan) and RAV4. TMMK was Toyota's first U.S. plant to manufacture hybrid electric vehicles (2006). Total Kentucky investment now exceeds $11 billion. The facility produces approximately 550,000 vehicles and 700,000 engines annually, supported by more than 400 suppliers nationwide.

📈 District Trajectory
  • Reading proficiency rose at all three levels from 2023–24 to 2024–25 — elementary +3pp, middle +9pp, high school +4pp
  • Math proficiency declined slightly at elementary (−1pp) while rising at middle (+3pp) and high school (+8pp)
  • Middle school Distinguished reading jumped to 28% — the highest of any level — largely driven by Scott County Middle School's strong performance
  • KDE reports statewide gains in 2024–25 ↗ for the third consecutive year under the current KSA system
⚠️ Equity Gap
  • Reading proficiency ranges from 60% (Northern Elementary) to ~28% (Garth, Southern Elementary) — a 32-point gap within the same district
  • Free/Reduced Lunch rates range from 32% (Eastern) to 68% (Garth) — strong correlation between economic disadvantage and lower scores
  • Georgetown Middle (58% FRL) scores 8–18pp below Scott County Middle (32% FRL) across subjects
  • Hispanic and ELL student performance at Eastern Elementary ranks in the bottom 10% of KY schools, despite the school's overall above-average ranking
🏫 Other Key Indicators
  • Graduation rate: ~93–94% (in line with KY state avg of 93% per 2023–24 KDE)
  • Chronic absenteeism: ~25% district-wide (all schools); exceeds KY pre-COVID baseline of ~18%
  • Student-teacher ratio: 16:1 (above KY avg of 15:1)
  • District rank: 80th of 159 KY districts (SchoolDigger 2024 composite)
  • Economically disadvantaged: 31.5% districtwide eligible for free/reduced lunch
  • ACT: All KY juniors take ACT at state expense; district-specific averages available via KY School Report Card ↗
📚 KSA System Notes
  • Grades tested: Reading & Math — Grades 3–8, 10; Science — Grades 4, 7, 11; Social Studies & Writing — Grades 5, 8, 11
  • Performance levels: Novice → Apprentice (≈grade level) → Proficient (above) → Distinguished (significantly above)
  • Accountability colors: Blue (Very High) · Green (High) · Yellow (Medium) · Orange (Low) · Red (Very Low)
  • KSA first administered spring 2022; prior system (K-PREP) used different scale — years before 2022 are not directly comparable
  • 2024–25 science used a redesigned assessment aligned to revised KAS standards — not comparable to prior science data
  • Full datasets back to 2011–12 available at KDE Open House ↗
